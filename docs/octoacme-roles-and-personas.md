# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business requirements and technical implementation. They analyze processes, validate requirements, and translate business needs into actionable specifications.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Draft process flows, user stories, and acceptance criteria
- Validate requirements with Product Managers and stakeholders
- Facilitate requirements workshops and discovery sessions
- Support Developers with clarification during implementation
- Assist in user acceptance testing and validation

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are clear, complete, and testable
- Reduce ambiguity in handoffs between business and technical teams
- Improve alignment between business objectives and technical solutions

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and user stories
- Process flow diagrams and data models
- Stakeholder workshops and validation sessions
- Clarification conversations with Developers during sprints

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers (PdM):** Collaborate closely to translate product vision into detailed requirements and acceptance criteria
- **Project Managers (PM):** Coordinate on scope, dependencies, and requirements documentation timelines
- **Developers:** Provide requirement clarifications, support estimation, and validate implemented solutions against business needs
- **UX Designer:** Partner on user journey mapping and ensure requirements align with user experience goals
- **QA Automation Engineer:** Define test scenarios and validation criteria based on requirements

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user-centered designs, ensuring interfaces and user journeys are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with brand standards.

### Responsibilities
- Design user interfaces, interactions, and journeys
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Ensure accessibility and responsive design standards
- Collaborate with Product and Engineering on design feasibility
- Participate in reviews and retrospective improvements

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive and accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and improve satisfaction metrics
- Maintain consistent design language across products

### Typical Communication
- Design specs, wireframes, and interactive prototypes
- Usability testing reports and insights
- Design critiques and collaborative review sessions
- Handoff documentation for Developers

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers (PdM):** Align on user needs, product vision, and design priorities
- **Developers:** Collaborate on design feasibility, provide design specs, and support implementation
- **Business Analyst:** Partner on user journey mapping and requirements validation
- **QA Automation Engineer:** Review designs for testability and validate implementation matches designs
- **Customer Support Specialist:** Gather feedback on user pain points and usability issues

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters (or Delivery Leads) facilitate agile teams, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile practices and continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate agile ceremonies (standups, planning, retrospectives, demos)
- Remove blockers and impediments for the team
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Update project board and ensure process adherence
- Ensure sprint cadence and rhythm are maintained
- Shield the team from external distractions

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and predictability
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Ensure smooth, sustainable delivery cadence

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of team ceremonies and meetings
- Blocker escalation and resolution tracking
- Sprint metrics and velocity reports
- Process improvement suggestions

### Key Interactions
- **Project Managers (PM):** Coordinate on delivery timelines, risk escalation, and stakeholder communication
- **Product Managers (PdM):** Facilitate backlog refinement and sprint planning with clear priorities
- **Developers:** Remove blockers, support estimation, and ensure team has what they need to deliver
- **All team members:** Coach on agile practices and facilitate collaboration across roles

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create, maintain, and improve documentation to ensure knowledge is accessible, accurate, and consistent across the organization.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain user guides, API documentation, and process docs
- Ensure consistency in terminology, style, and formatting
- Collaborate with stakeholders to gather and validate content
- Support knowledge transfer and onboarding documentation
- Review and improve existing documentation based on feedback
- Maintain documentation standards and templates

### Goals
- Deliver clear, accurate, and useful documentation
- Reduce time to onboard new team members
- Ensure documentation stays current with product changes

### Typical Communication
- Documentation pull requests and change proposals
- Content review sessions with subject matter experts
- Documentation style guides and standards
- User feedback analysis and doc improvement plans

### Key Interactions
- **Developers:** Gather technical details, review API docs, and ensure accuracy of implementation guides
- **Product Managers (PdM):** Align on feature documentation and user-facing content
- **Project Managers (PM):** Coordinate documentation timelines and deliverables
- **Business Analyst:** Review requirements documentation for clarity and consistency
- **Customer Support Specialist:** Incorporate common questions and pain points into documentation

---

## QA Automation Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Automation Engineers design, implement, and maintain automated test suites to ensure product quality, reliability, and faster feedback cycles.

### Responsibilities
- Implement and maintain automated test frameworks
- Create test plans and test cases for new features
- Identify, document, and track defects
- Coordinate with Developers on quality improvement initiatives
- Monitor test coverage and identify gaps
- Run regression testing before releases

### Goals
- Increase test coverage and automation rate
- Reduce manual testing effort and time-to-feedback
- Catch defects earlier in the development cycle

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and coverage reports
- Bug reports with reproduction steps
- Test automation framework documentation
- Quality metrics and trend analysis

### Key Interactions
- **Developers:** Collaborate on testability, review code for quality, and coordinate bug fixes
- **Product Managers (PdM):** Validate acceptance criteria and ensure quality aligns with customer expectations
- **Business Analyst:** Review requirements for testability and define test scenarios
- **UX Designer:** Validate implementation matches design specifications
- **Project Managers (PM):** Report on quality status, testing progress, and release readiness

---

## Customer Support Specialist

### Role Summary
Customer Support Specialists provide user assistance, gather feedback from end users, and serve as the voice of the customer to inform product improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Respond to customer inquiries and provide solutions
- Document common issues and create knowledge base articles
- Escalate bugs and feature requests to Product Managers
- Provide user feedback to inform product decisions
- Track customer satisfaction and support metrics
- Participate in user acceptance testing when relevant

### Goals
- Deliver excellent customer experience and satisfaction
- Reduce time to resolution for customer issues
- Provide actionable feedback to improve the product

### Typical Communication
- Support ticket responses and resolution updates
- Escalation reports to Product and Engineering
- Knowledge base articles and FAQs
- Customer feedback summaries and trend reports

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers (PdM):** Escalate user feedback, feature requests, and pain points to inform roadmap
- **Developers:** Report bugs with clear reproduction steps and collaborate on fixes
- **Technical Writer:** Provide input on documentation gaps and common user questions
- **UX Designer:** Share insights on user struggles and usability issues
- **QA Automation Engineer:** Validate bug fixes and assist with user acceptance testing
